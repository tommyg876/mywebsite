Solar Business
Financial Performance
$3.5M revenue, $500k EBITDA
Ed's friend. Low penetration in central coast. Does all the sales himself. Has 5 installers. Started after Civil Engineering degree.
Scaling Challenges
Wants to scale but doesn't know how. Has not installed or set up a CRM process so has no idea deal conversion stages, tracking & optimising that.
Ed thinks he should hire a lot of sales reps, pump some ads & free himself up.
Industry Insights
Hormozi's View: Divested from solar business, cashflow business, with no enterprise value because no recurring revenue within the business. Can takeover staff, but no built-in revenue.
Energy Maestro thesis: nation-wide solar companies don't really work because the local guy wins. The guy that people know & know is only one call away wins. They looked at doing roll-ups, acquiring other solar businesses but ultimately opted not to do it. Economics didn't make sense.
Counter-argument: You could always create licensing model with chains in local towns & advertise to them. There are solar companies doing $300-400M in USA as well.
Ecom Supplements Company
Background
Started 5 years ago. Charlie (founder) has experience doing biochemistry degree at Uni & then running creative agency. They wanted to build a supplement company using those marketing skills.
Distribution Strategy
Found large-distribution retailers as first acquisition methods. Coles, Woolworths & recently closed Priceline as first Pharmacy deal.
Since, they tinkered with Meta & had poor results. Have done content which doesn't drive much. Expanded to retail distributors in Vietnam & South-East Asia. Think they could be predominant supplement store in APAC.
Product Focus
90% of sales come from sleep, hydration & energy
All of these already existed & were copied from the US. Mainly target millennial females, not dudes. All marketing is done by female. Female mainly buy fancy supplements like collagen, sleep. No masculine supplements like creatine, protein powder.
Major Challenges
Cash Flow Issues: Pay manufacturing company upfront. Wait 6 months for them to be made. Coles/Woolworths pay them 6 months later. So, they theoretically could be profitable. But, this means they have to dilute equity, raise debt to grow because payback mechanism is bad.
Lost Distribution: Lost Coles as major distribution partner. Had 3rd party vendor handling the arrangement. They didn't control it. So, they lost it.
No Customer Data: Don't get any data from coles/woolworths around who the customer is, demographics, wealth, gender, age etc. So, have no idea how to position the brand.
Ads Agency — Getting to $15k/month in 18 months
Initial Strategy
Cold Called 50-100 times per day until got clients. GMB numbers with iPhone. No automation system. Found home-cleaners & eventually did roofers as well.
$100M Offer. Don't get paid until they got results. Resulted in extremely high churn at the beginning. No cleaners got great results, so they churned.
Product-Market Fit
Started with Residential home cleaners. Pivoted to roofers & found product-market fit. Found the one franchise which did really well. 7 customers & did speaking event from partnership with Shawn. Was booking $1M in contract quotes. Hasn't closed a single deal.
Channel Optimization
Switched acquisition from cold outreach to paid advertising. Works better. Cold outreach = Unqualified Leads & Josh would really have sell it, meaning Joel had hard time living up to expectations.
Pricing = $1.5k/month for the whole service not including ad-spend
Grew Accounting Practice from $1M to $2M in 1 year
Acquisition
Rob bought the business from Ray in 2024. Paid $1M for $1M revenue business with ~20-25% profit margins. Earn-out overtime as Ray stays as one of the accountants of the business. Rob put up his house as collateral.
Growth Strategy
Rob doubled the business in a year
Spent $3-5k/month on Google Ads. Used agency then fired them. Spends same on Bark leads — affiliate partnership. Tried cold email & paid $10k for the month, only signed 10 customers so stopped the process.
Operations
They mainly work on volume & try to bring as many people in as humanly possible. SMB focused. $500-750/month as sweet spot. Extremely productised with no custom work.
Tries to optimise operations by incorporating AI into their workflows. As well as Zapier automations.
Expansion Opportunity
Roll-up Strategy: Continue buying accounting businesses & rolling them up onto Proactive. Grow via acquisition. Incredibly fragmented market with 200k+ accounting businesses in Australia. Most are small shops of 1-5 person with no marketing & rely on recurring revenue.
HealthTech Startup in SF
Funding & Team
$4M seed before he even had an idea in 2022. Then, raised $30M Series A in March 2025.
Jacob ran 2 companies prior (launch-house, commsor) & JP Morgan. Kev built dev-ops tool. Max ran angel-investing syndicate & private community. Initial fund-raising was on Jacob's back.
Team = 10-15 growth members, 5 EAs, 10 engineers, 3 founders.
Current Performance
$500k ARR
Investor backs space & founders. Most were team, friends & employees. Virtually, no customers that were broadly organic.
Product Offering
Four Main Services:
(1) Marketplace of curated supplements. Buy in-bulk so members get discount versus normal site.
(2) Concierge with AI RAG chatbot & interface connecting with message bird
(3) Blood-test with white-label partner & dashboard showing results
(4) Other white-label diagnostic services like dexa scans, microbiome testing, CGM testing
Operational Challenges
Had no consistent customer acquisition methods. While had a tonne of content views, don't think they drove sign-ups. 1.2M impressions on X, LI on launch day. Most from Australia. Meta Ads CAC doesn't work because product is too expensive & nebulous.
Lots of pointless meetings to discuss irrelevant things to hide constraint being lack of engineers. Meeting about cancellation/subscription notice policy. Lots of memos & writing from Max about customer experience messaging, future of healthcare, rx & diagnostic services. Have to find ways of staying busy. Probably doesn't need 3 founders.
